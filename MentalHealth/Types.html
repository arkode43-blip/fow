Types of Mental disorders

Generalized Anxiety disorder

PTSD is a condition caused by a traumatic event either by experiencing trauma, or witnessing it. After the traumatic event the person may have constant flashbacks, nightmares, or thoughts about the event

Mood Disorder

Persistent Depressive Mental Disorder is a long-term form of depression (usually mild) that causes constant feelings of sadness, emptiness, and or hopelessness in the patient.

Eating Disorder

Anorexia Nervosa is a condition where an individual restricts food/caloric intake (self-starvation). They also fear gaining weight, and have a distorted self-image.

Gender Disorder

Gender Dysphoria is a condition characterized by a feeling of distress happening when a individuals gender identity differs from their gender at birth.

Psychotic Disorder

Schizophrenia is a serious psychiatric disorder characterized by hallucinations, delusions, or disorganized speech. It significantly declines your ability to work/daily life.

Personality Disorder

Paranoid Personality Disorder is a condition characterized by paranoia which causes mistrust or suspicion of others. People with this disorder often believe that others are trying to demean, harm, or threaten them.

Impulse Control disorder

Kleptomania is a condition characterized by the desire to steal things. People with this condition know that Stealing is wrong, but they can't stop themselves.

Factitious Disorder

Munchausen Syndrome is a serious mental health disorder in which an individual deceives others by pretending to be sick. Usually they fake symptoms, get sick on purpose, or hurt themselves.
